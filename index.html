About

Xiaoya Wang is a second-year MA student in Applied Linguistics at Teachers College, Columbia University. She holds a bachelor's degree in Business English. Her research interests focus on language assessment, computational linguistics, and corpus linguistics.

Before joining Teachers College, Xiaoya was a visiting student at UC Berkeley for a year, specializing in Phonetics and Phonology. During her time there, she worked as a research assistant on projects related to Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Large Language Models (LLMs) through the Undergraduate Research Apprentice Program (URAP) under the supervision of Dr. Zehlia Babaci-Wilhite.

Research Interests

  • Language assessment & automated essay scoring
  • Computational linguistics for L2 writing
  • Corpus linguistics in language education
  • AI-mediated feedback in writing instruction

Current Projects

  • Systematic review: Constructs of second language writing in automated essay scoring (AES).
  • AWE project: Teachers’ mediation in AI-based revision processes.
  • Corpus study: Linguistic features of AI-generated vs. human-generated feedback.
